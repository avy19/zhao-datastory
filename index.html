Avy's Data-Story

Data Collection

For my data collection project, I tracked my use of chatting platforms on the computer. Every time I sent a message on one of three differentapps — namely Discord, Skype, and GroupMe — I made a note of it in my Google Sheets.

I not only copy-pasted the content of the message but also recorded the following information:
✑  what platform I'd sent the message on

✑  which group chat I'd sent the message to

✑  how I engaged with the platform (e.g. if I clicked on a link or viewed an image in the chatroom)

✑  whether I was pinged/notified, or if I checked the platform on my own

✑  my mood at the time of sending my message

✑  any non-word text I used

✑  any other tasks I should've been doing besides or instead of responding to a group chat

Although I did not end up using all of the different types of data I gathered about my online chatting activity for the following section, they were all nevertheless informative about how I chat online. In doing this data collection, I learned a little more about myself and about the presence of digital data in my life.

Specifically, I became more aware that chatting platforms were my primary method of communicating with people on a daily basis; I use it very often throughout my day simply because it's the quickest and easiest way of contacting several people at once, letting me hold several conversations at the sametime. As such, it's both efficient and convenient.

Data Visualization

In the process of turning the data collection into an image for this data visualization project, I first learned to try different ideas for interpretation, aiming for something that would be unique but also relatively easy to understand.

Ultimately, I opted for a simple color scheme and clear layout with only a few overlapping parts to promote transparency. The clearly named vases for the flowers efficiently represent the fact that my data was spread over the course of five days, too. I also ensured that the legend included all the information needed to understand the differnt parts of the visualization.

I learned a lot about data visualization construction in this project, and it had a lot more levels than I initially thought. Even though the visualization is competely creative in terms of form — different from all typical bar or line graphs — the creator still needs to keep the type of data in mind in order to make the illustration easy to interpret.
